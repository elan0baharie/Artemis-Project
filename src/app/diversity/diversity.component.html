Diversity & Inclusion Assessment

This tool is meant to enable organizations to benchmark their current diversity and inclusion activities and provide guidance in identifying areas to drive change. Companies that score high demonstrate they are performing at levels considered to be industry best practices while companies that score low demonstrate they have some work to do.

Commitment to Diversity

The CEO is a sponsor a diversity and inclusion program.





We have an official definition and shared understanding of diversity and inclusion.





Diversity and inclusion is a core value and goal for our organization that are tied to business objectives and strategic goals.





Our managers / administrators can easily describe why diversity and inclusion are important for our organization.





We have conducted a pay equity analysis in the last year.





We have identified our organization's’ gender breakdown by role, level and department in the last year.





Diversity and inclusion competence considerations are integrated into our organization's strategic plan (including recruiting, hiring, retention, and promotion).

Employment Practices

We are successful in recruiting and retaining Board leaders, senior management, and other leadership positions from diverse backgrounds with expertise and ability to execute our strategic plan.





Job descriptions include diversity and inclusion statements.





We have increased our percentage of diverse employee hires over the same period last year.





We review metrics to evaluate and assess our workforce diversity and inclusion efforts.





We have documented processes that promotes a diverse pool of candidates in our hiring, recruiting, retention, and promotion.





We have a senior level official who oversees and direct diversity efforts.





We have a diversity/inclusion committee that meets on a regular basis.





We have developed a supplier diversity policy that provides fair opportunity for minority-owned and women-owned businesses.





Our employees have participated in at least one diversity/inclusion training in the past 12 months.





We have regular employee engagement surveys with feedback loops for action in place.





Orientation for new employees addresses our diversity and inclusion beliefs and policies.





We have provided accessibility for non-English speakers and individuals with disabilities.

Communication

We are transparent about our diversity and inclusion activities and make information available to our employees, suppliers, consumers, and the public on our website.





We regularly communicate our diversity and inclusion metrics and progress.





We do not shy away from talking about diversity and inclusion within our organization.

Culture

Our employees feel valued.





Employees are encouraged to be themselves.





Employees think outcomes, processes, and communications are fair.





Leaders are open to bad news.





Employees are encouraged to ask questions.





Leaders are open to new ideas and innovative approaches.





Employees have influence over their job responsibilities.





Our teams embrace change well.





Employees are given stretch opportunities.





Employees who are willing to learn new knowledge or skills are valued.





Work-life balance for employees is a priority for managers and leaders.





Flexible working arrangement are available.





We have family-friendly policies in place including extended parental leave and family emergency days.
