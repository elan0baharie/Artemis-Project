Diversity & Inclusion Assessment

This tool is meant to enable organizations to benchmark their current diversity and inclusion activities and provide guidance in identifying areas to drive change. Companies that score high demonstrate they are performing at levels considered to be industry best practices while companies that score low demonstrate they have some work to do.

Commitment (leadership, accountability and measurement)

The CEO is a sponsor a diversity and inclusion program.





We have a dedicated employee overseeing and managing the company’s diversity performance.





We have a senior level official who oversees and directs diversity efforts.





We have a diversity/inclusion committee that meets on a regular basis.





Creating a diverse and inclusive workplace is a goal that has been integrated into the organization’s strategic plan.





A pay equity analysis was conducted in the last year.





The organization has developed and established metrics for measuring the diversity of our workforce.





The organization has measured its gender breakdown by role, level, and department in the last year.





Diversity metrics are reviewed on a regular basis to evaluate and assess the progress of diversity and inclusion efforts.





Diversity and inclusion competence considerations are integrated into our organization's strategic plan (including recruiting, hiring, retention, and promotion).

Cultivation (recruiting, developing and retaining talent)

The organization is successful in recruiting and retaining Board leaders, senior management, and other leadership positions from diverse backgrounds with the expertise and ability to execute our strategic plan.





Job descriptions include diversity and inclusion statements.





The organization has increased the percentage of diverse employee hires over the same period last year.





The organization has documented processes that promotes a diverse pool of candidates in our hiring, recruiting, retention, and promotion.





The organization has tailored its diversity and inclusion interventions to individual sites and markets.





Employees have participated in at least one diversity and inclusion or cultural sensitivity training session in the past 12 months.





Unconscious bias training has been completed by all managers and senior leaders in the organization.





The organization conducts regular employee engagement surveys with feedback loops for action in place.





Orientation for new employees addresses the organization’s diversity and inclusion beliefs and policies.





Diversity and inclusion policies and beliefs have informed the organization’s learning and development strategy.





Promotion decisions are monitored to ensure that they are fair and that all employees are being given the opportunity to advance.





The organization/work place is accessible to non-English speakers and individuals with disabilities.





The organization supports and enables the formation of Employee Resource Groups and has feedback loops for action in place.





We have developed a supplier diversity policy that provides equal opportunity for all suppliers, regardless of gender, race, age, education, disability, religion, sexual orientation, background, or family situation.





Next Section

Communication

The organization has an official definition and shared understanding of diversity and inclusion that is shared and understood by employees at all levels.





Employees at all levels can easily describe why diversity and inclusion are important for our organization.





The organization is transparent about its diversity and inclusion activities and makes information available to its employees, suppliers, consumers, and the public on its website.





The organization regularly communicates its diversity and inclusion metrics and progress.





The organization has an open door policy when it comes to issues diversity and inclusion related issues or concerns.

Culture

Employees feel valued.





Employees feel like their achievements have been fairly recognized and rewarded.





Employees are encouraged to be themselves.





Employees think outcomes, processes, and communications are fair and respectful.





Leaders encourage honest feedback and constructive criticism.





Employees are encouraged to ask questions.





Leaders are open to new ideas and innovative approaches.





Employees have influence over their job responsibilities.





Our teams embrace change well.





Employees are given stretch opportunities.





All employees are given clear guidance on career development opportunities and potential promotion pathways.





Employees who are willing to learn new knowledge or skills are valued.





Work-life balance for employees is a priority for managers and leaders.





Flexible working arrangements are available.





Family-friendly policies are in place including extended parental leave and family emergency days.
